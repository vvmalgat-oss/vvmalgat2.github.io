Analysis and Work

Detailed explanation of the Tableau analysis and dashboard development.

Project Objective

The objective was to assess the financial quality of S&P 500 companies using a composite Quality Score, enabling comparison across companies and sectors.

Data Preparation

Data was cleaned and prepared in SQL by standardising financial ratios, handling missing values and validating calculations prior to visualisation.

Dashboard Design

The dashboard was designed to move from sector level performance to individual company insights using interactive filters and customised tooltips.

Key Insights

1. Leaders sit in Communication Services, Materials, and Energy.

2. Verizon, LyondellBasell, AES, and Ford rank at the top. They score well on low P/E, steady yield, and fair profits.

3. The worst scores are in tech firms like Autodesk, Synopsys, Ansys, PTC, Intuit, and Netflix. High P/E and P/S destroy their scores, even when book value looks strong.

4. Small groups like Agricultural Products (Bunge) and Advertising (IPG) look best on paper. But with only 1â€“2 firms, this is not a broad truth.

5. Valuation rules the model. P/E, P/S, and yield drive most of the score. EBITDA adds little.

Recommendations

1. Overweight Communication Services (Verizon, AT&T). Yields are high, and P/E is low.

2. Be careful with growth tech. They look bad in this model, but if you scale by sector, some may rise.

3. Do not chase small sectors like Advertising or Agricultural. They are too thin to anchor a plan.

4. Re-test with tweaks. Split no-dividend vs. missing data. Cap ratios by sector. This will give fairer scores and less bias against growth.
